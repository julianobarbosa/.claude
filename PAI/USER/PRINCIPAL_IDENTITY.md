## Worldview

Most engineering complexity is fashion, not necessity — teams adopt Kubernetes, service meshes, and elaborate IaC because the market signals seriousness, when the real problem would yield to simpler tools. Simplicity is the harder, more honest choice. Cloud cost is rarely a technical problem; it's a cultural and accountability problem wearing a technical wrapper. Geography matters: a discipline imported wholesale from another market without translation leaves practitioners feeling perpetually behind when they're actually working a different problem. Craft — the patience to build something that holds up — is a counter-cultural stance in an industry that rewards velocity over durability.

- **Cloud cost discipline doesn't scale through tools alone — it scales through engineering accountability.** Dashboards without owners produce reports, not savings.
